Thoughts
ParkourState, and the Jump states are still pretty poor. DropDown isn't working. Jump from under ledge is still failing. 

The common theme with most of these states that aren't working is that they have environment pre-conditions, and manipulate the character on a semi-scripted movement path from a semi-arbitrary start point to a target point. It's proving quite difficult to keep these looking clean, smooth, and controllable/intuitive though. 

There's a few common goals here. 
- respect player intent as much as possible. Pressing arrows indicates an intent to go in a certain direction. Inputting a command indicates an intent to carry out a movement, either now or later. Use of controller buffer / move priority should reflect this
- make player movement predictable
- make player movement continuous. 
    - Avoid frequent jerky movements
    - preserve normal velocity. e.g. walking over and ducking under random obstacles should feel as smooth as just walking over flat ground, occasionally slowed by crouching. 
    - prevent the player from running into corners, getting blocked by overhands, etc, when a movement state is capable of preventing it
- don't fight the player
    - make sure there's a way to do everything the player can reasonably do. for instance, it should be possible to approach an outcrop without triggering a vault, but it should also be possible to correctly trigger a vault without needing significantly good timing. 

Right now, there's maybe three states planned that require guided movement, with the ParkourState being the archetypal one. I'd like to try to really nail ParkourState, and then use the ideas from that to quickly recreate the other similar states, like LedgePull, LedgeDrop, CoveredJump, etc. 

ParkourState unifies the previous VaultState and DuckState, which handled smoothly vaulting over blocks and smoothly ducking under ledges respectively. I felt I had to combine them, because under some edge cases such as climbing a stairway of blocks into a covered tunnel, both need to activate smoothly. 

The basic idea of ParkourState:

A) the player is moving to the left or right
B) they run into an obstacle, like a one block height platform, or a slightly lower than head height tunnel, or stairs leading up or down into a tunnel

E.g.
    
OOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOO
OOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXX
OOOOXXXXXXXXXXOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOO
XXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOO
XXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXX
XXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXX

C) They either moving fast enough, or holding up/down to trigger state entry

D) I then push the player onto a movement path which keeps them moving over/under/into the obstacle without interupting their movement. I also add constraints/phantom surfaces to prevent them from running into corners etc. 

- Although the path is planned/scripted, it should feel like a continuation of the walking state. This means that it should be completely interruptable, by letting go of the arrow key, jumping, etc, and it shouldn't induce anything the feels unnatural, like artificially fast jumps or drops, strange looking slowdowns or speedups, etc. 

- Right now I'm using a bunch of random scripted forces to generate this effect, but it's painful, and I'd really like a more general method. 

- There's lots of ideas. One medium complicated one would be to build a Bezier curve system, which takes in the players inital position and velocity, figures out their target endpoint when they exit parkour state (up or down a block, slightly crouched, etc), and calculates a path which is smooth, preserves horizontal velocity, etc. I could then just use forces to push the player along that path. 
- Another is to take the phantom constraints idea further. Right now I'm adding floating surfaces to corners during the parkourstate, so that the character can't unintentionally run into edges. I could take that further, so that normal player movement, combined with extra constraints, produces the parkour effect. I'd probably need to tweak collisions, force from movement, etc, to keep movement looking continuous. 

In general this is a hard problem, but I need a principled, extendable system for producing these effects.
